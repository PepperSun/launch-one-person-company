$launch-one-person-company

When you have a raw one-person company idea, this skill gives you the global view needed to see feasible execution paths. It looks across customers, business model, distribution, operations, risk, and sequencing, then turns the result into a visual action map.

Core Capability

The skill turns idea-level uncertainty into a whole-system execution view, then narrows that view into a practical action map.

Idea Intake

Turn a loose idea into launch context

The skill identifies the minimum facts needed to reason about the business without pretending every decision is already settled.

Global View

See the whole execution landscape

It checks customer path, revenue model, launch stage, operating stack, distribution, compliance, and risk together.

Feasibility Filter

Separate possible from premature

It keeps safe validation moving while marking blocked, high-risk, or source-dependent work clearly.

Action Map

Convert the view into a map

The final artifact is a visual `00-action-map.html` with ordered action IDs, dependencies, risks, and next steps.

What The Skill Proves

A good launch assistant should not only answer the idea. It should reveal the execution field around the idea.

It creates strategic visibility

The founder can see customer discovery, offer design, operations, distribution, and risk in one view.

It makes feasibility explicit

The map separates what can be done now from what needs facts, sources, or professional review.

It produces the action map

The final output is a scannable map of action IDs, dependencies, risks, and next steps.
