# Social Account Operations

Social operations are part of launch readiness when public trust, community access, or founder-led distribution can accelerate validation. They are not always the primary path.

## Account Strategy

| Option | Best For | Advantages | Disadvantages | Default |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Founder account | Consulting, productized service, early SaaS, AI tools | Trust, personal expertise, easier conversations | Tied to founder identity, needs consistency | Default for most early OPCs |
| Brand account | Product-led SaaS, digital product, future team | Separates product from founder | Harder to build trust from zero | Use when product name matters |
| Both | Clear positioning and enough time to maintain both | Separates thought leadership and support | Doubles workload | Delay until a repeatable cadence exists |
| Neither | Warm intros, direct interviews, stealth validation | Fastest path to real conversations | Less public proof | Use when ICP is unclear |

## Channel Selection

| Channel | Strong For | Weak For | Risk / Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | B2B, consulting, productized service, professional buyers | Developer-native or anonymous audiences | Avoid exaggerated claims and spammy automation |
| X | AI, indie hacking, build-in-public, developer tools | Local services or conservative enterprise buyers | High noise; needs consistent voice |
| Reddit | Niche communities, problem discovery, candid feedback | Direct promotion | Follow subreddit rules; contribute before asking |
| Hacker News | Technical founders, developer tools, SaaS lessons | Generic self-promotion | Be specific, useful, and transparent |
| Indie Hackers | Bootstrapped SaaS, digital products | Non-indie audiences | Good for learning and feedback |
| YouTube / TikTok | Demonstrable workflows, education, creator-led offers | Quiet B2B validation | Higher production burden |
| Newsletter | Ongoing expertise and owned audience | Immediate validation from zero | Slow to start |
| Slack / Discord communities | Niche B2B or technical groups | Broadcast marketing | Follow community norms |
| Cold email | B2B direct validation | Consumer or community-first products | Must be targeted, compliant, and respectful |

## 30-Day Default Plan

Recommend no more than two primary channels for the first 30 days.

Default cadence:

- 10-20 targeted conversations per week.
- 2-3 useful public posts per week if a public channel is chosen.
- 15 minutes per day of community replies or comment participation.
- Weekly review of qualified conversations, demos, waitlist signups, and paid intent.

## Profile Checklist

- Clear one-line positioning.
- Founder credibility signal.
- Customer segment and problem.
- Link to waitlist, booking page, email, or website when available.
- Pinned post or featured item explaining the current offer or learning goal.
- No unsupported regulated, financial, medical, legal, or guaranteed-outcome claims.

## Content Pillars

Choose 3-4:

- Problem observations from customer discovery.
- Workflow teardown or before/after examples.
- Founder build notes and constraints.
- Customer education.
- Case studies or anonymized lessons.
- Product or service demos.
- Common mistakes and decision guides.
- Transparent launch updates.

## Community Participation SOP

1. Observe rules, tone, and repeated pain points before posting.
2. Reply helpfully before asking for anything.
3. Ask discovery questions before pitching.
4. Move qualified conversations to email, CRM, waitlist, or booking only after relevance is clear.
5. Log every qualified conversation in `09-crm-pipeline.csv`.
6. Avoid mass posting the same message across communities.

## Lead Capture

Connect social activity to one simple next step:

- Book a discovery call.
- Join a waitlist.
- Reply by email.
- Request a demo.
- Fill a short problem survey.
- Pre-order or reserve a pilot.

## Metrics

Track:

- Replies from target customers.
- Qualified conversations.
- Discovery calls booked.
- Waitlist signups.
- Demo requests.
- Trial or pilot starts.
- Paid conversions.
- Content-to-lead ratio.
- Channel time spent.

## When Social Is Not Priority

Do not prioritize social if:

- The ICP is unclear and interviews through warm intros are faster.
- The offer is sensitive or regulated and public claims are risky.
- The buyer is reachable through direct outbound or existing relationships.
- The founder cannot maintain even a light cadence.
- A niche community prohibits promotion and the founder has not built trust.
