# U.S. External SOPs

Use this reference before writing registration, tax, licensing, BOI, privacy, AI disclosure, banking, or other external-process SOPs.

## Research Rules

Before writing a current U.S. SOP, browse official sources. Prefer:

- State business portal or secretary of state.
- IRS for EIN, self-employment, estimated tax, and federal tax topics.
- FinCEN for BOI status.
- State department of revenue or tax agency.
- City or county license portals.
- FTC for consumer protection, endorsements, advertising, privacy, and AI claims.
- State attorney general or privacy regulator.
- Industry regulator or licensing board.
- Official vendor documentation for platform-specific account or policy requirements.

If official sources conflict or are unavailable, state the conflict and mark the SOP as requiring confirmation.

## SOP Format

Use this structure for each external task:

```markdown
### SOP: [Task Name]

**Status:** Ready / Blocked / Needs professional confirmation
**Risk level:** Low / Medium / High
**Official sources:** [source name](url)

**Prerequisites**

```

## Common SOPs

### Entity Formation Decision

Do not automatically recommend formation. Compare delay, sole proprietorship, single-member LLC, and corporation when relevant. Explain costs, liability, tax/admin burden, credibility, bank/payment needs, and customer contract needs.

### State Registration

State-specific instructions require the selected state. If the state is unknown, block this SOP and generate a state-selection decision support section.

### EIN

Use IRS official sources. Explain when an EIN is useful or required, what information is commonly needed, and what the user receives. Do not imply an EIN creates an entity.

### Business Bank Account

Describe typical prerequisites such as entity documents, EIN when applicable, operating agreement when applicable, ID, address, and beneficial-owner information. Link official bank/vendor requirements only when recommending a specific bank.

### State Tax and Sales Tax Checks

Do not assert sales tax obligations without current state-specific verification. Provide a check process through the state tax agency and identify triggers such as selling taxable digital goods, services, software, subscriptions, or crossing economic nexus thresholds.

### Local Licenses

Check city and county portals when the user has a location. If location is unknown, mark as blocked or generic.

### BOI / FinCEN

Always verify current FinCEN status from official sources before writing. Rules and injunctions can change. State whether the user's entity type appears in scope only after checking current guidance.

### Privacy, AI, and Consumer Protection

For AI and software businesses, check data collection, privacy policy, AI claims, advertising claims, endorsements, automated decision impact, data retention, and customer authorization. Escalate regulated outputs involving health, finance, legal, employment, housing, credit, children, biometrics, or sensitive personal data.

### Insurance and Professional Review

Recommend evaluation of general liability, E&O or professional liability, cyber insurance, and industry-specific coverage when risk or customer requirements justify it. Do not represent insurance as a substitute for compliance.
