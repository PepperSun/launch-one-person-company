# Archetypes

Classify the business into one primary archetype and, when helpful, one secondary archetype.

## B2B SaaS or Micro-SaaS

Best fit: software solves a repeatable business workflow for a narrow customer segment.

Launch focus:

- Validate the painful workflow and buying trigger.
- Create a landing page, waitlist, demo, or concierge MVP.
- Plan subscription, usage-based, or annual pricing.
- Prepare privacy, terms, support, billing, cancellation, and data-processing basics.
- Expect security questionnaires or DPA requests for larger B2B customers.

Default path: customer interviews, manual concierge workflow, landing page, CRM pipeline, Stripe or equivalent only after pricing is credible.

## AI Tool, AI Agent, or Automation

Best fit: AI output or automation is central to the value proposition.

Launch focus:

- Identify what data enters the AI system and what decisions the output influences.
- Check model/API provider terms, data retention, privacy, customer authorization, and regulated-output risk.
- Add AI limitations, human review, and responsibility boundaries to website and customer materials.
- Prefer narrow use cases with measurable workflow improvement.

Default path: scoped pilot, human-in-the-loop delivery, clear disclaimers, no sensitive-data intake until the risk review is complete.

## Productized Service

Best fit: repeatable service with fixed scope, package, delivery steps, and price.

Launch focus:

- Define packages, deliverables, exclusions, revision limits, timeline, and onboarding materials.
- Use proposal, agreement, invoice, payment terms, and CRM from day one.
- Create service SOPs before scaling delivery or outsourcing.

Default path: sell one narrow package manually, document delivery, then standardize.

## Consulting or Freelance Expert Business

Best fit: the founder sells expertise, diagnosis, strategy, implementation, or advisory work.

Launch focus:

- Clarify positioning, proof, advisory scope, rate model, and referral/outbound path.
- Use a simple personal site or profile unless a full website helps credibility.
- Consider E&O or professional liability insurance for higher-stakes advice.

Default path: warm network and direct outreach first, lightweight profile, service agreement, invoice tracking.

## Digital Product, Template, Course, or Paid Knowledge Product

Best fit: the founder sells reusable knowledge, templates, files, training, or community access.

Launch focus:

- Validate topic and willingness to pay before building full content.
- Decide platform, refund terms, copyright/license terms, email capture, and sales tax check.
- Use audience, community, newsletter, or search distribution.

Default path: pre-sell or waitlist, outline minimum product, publish sample value, then launch through a simple checkout platform.
